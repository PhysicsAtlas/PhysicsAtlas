Macroscopic World

1. Classical Mechanics

The study of bodies in motion and the forces acting upon them, as defined by Sir Isaac Newton.

[attachment_0](attachment)
F = m × a
  • Kinematics: Describing motion without considering its causes.
  • Dynamics: Exploring why things move (Forces, Torque, Energy).
  • Gravitation: Every mass attracts every other mass in the universe.
Energy & Heat

2. Thermodynamics

Focuses on how energy is transferred and how it affects matter.

[attachment_1](attachment)

The First Law: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.

ΔU = Q - W

The Second Law: The total entropy (disorder) of an isolated system always increases over time.

Oscillations

3. Waves & Optics

Waves carry energy through space. Optics deals specifically with light (electromagnetic waves).

[attachment_2](attachment)
  • Reflection: Light bouncing off surfaces.
  • Refraction: Light bending as it enters a new medium.
  • Diffraction: Waves bending around corners or through slits.
v = f × λ
Fields & Forces

4. Electromagnetism

Unified by James Clerk Maxwell, this field explains electricity, magnetism, and light.

[attachment_3](attachment)

Moving electric charges create magnetic fields, and changing magnetic fields create electric currents (Induction).

High Velocity

5. Special Relativity

Proposed by Albert Einstein, it fundamentally changed our understanding of time and space.

[attachment_4](attachment)
  • Time Dilation: Time slows down as you move faster.
  • Mass-Energy Equivalence: Matter can be converted into pure energy.
E = m × c²
Atomic Scale

6. Quantum Mechanics

In the world of atoms, things are "quantized" (broken into discrete packets) and probabilistic.

Wave-Particle Duality: Electrons and light behave as both particles and waves depending on how you look at them.

Δx Δp ≥ h / 4π
Fundamental Reality

7. Particle Physics

Investigates the building blocks of the universe using the Standard Model.

[attachment_5](attachment)
  • Quarks: The tiny components of Protons and Neutrons.
  • Leptons: Including Electrons and Neutrinos.
  • Bosons: Force carriers, like the Photon (Light) and the Higgs Boson (Mass).
